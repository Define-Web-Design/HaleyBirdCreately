**Full-Scope UI/UX Audit and Revision Prompt:**

Please conduct a comprehensive, **full-scope UI/UX analysis** of the application (both web and mobile) to identify and fix all issues while aligning with modern, intuitive, and seamless design principles. The app should prioritize accessibility, efficiency, and user experience. Below are the detailed steps:

---

### **1. Navigation Bar Adjustments (Top Toolbar)**

- **Notifications Icon:**
  - Remove all duplicate notification icons. There must be a **single notifications icon** with a clean blue indicator that represents unread notifications.
  - Align the notification icon creatively and harmoniously next to the profile button. It must look **connected and synchronized** with its surroundings, not floating out of place.

- **Profile Button:**
  - Resize and reformat the profile circle into a **clickable button**. This button should trigger a **drop-down menu** containing essential profile actions:
    - Profile
    - Settings
    - Log Out
  - Ensure that the button’s design matches the rest of the top bar (consistent spacing, alignment, and modern visuals).

---

### **2. Link & Button Functionality**

- **Interactive Elements Audit:**
  - Verify all info icons, view buttons, and linked buttons are not just visually present but also correctly **linked to pages or actions**.
  - Test every button across the app, ensuring it performs its expected functionality.
  - For missing or unclear actions, decide intuitively:
    - Use quick views (in modals or expandable areas) for lightweight interactions.
    - Create dedicated pages (like a “canvas” with room to iterate and refine) for complex content.

- **Navigation and Page Access:**
  - Ensure every call-to-action (CTA) button navigates users to the proper pages.
  - Detect and resolve any orphaned or missing pages (e.g., landing pages referenced in the UI but not implemented).
  - Cross-check that no links or buttons lead to dead ends or incorrect destinations.

- **Accessibility Integration:**
  - Every interactive element must have hidden but accessible descriptions (e.g., screen reader texts using ARIA labels and `.visually-hidden` CSS). Maintain a clean UI for non-accessibility users.

---

### **3. Comprehensive UI/UX Consistency Check**

- **Page Layout and Consistency:**
  - Confirm typography, color schemes, and spacing are consistent across the app.
  - Inspect modals, menus, panels, and interactive elements to ensure they align with the overall design language.

- **Responsive Design Audit:**
  - Test the app on desktops, tablets, and various mobile devices using responsive design tools.
  - Verify that all UI components adapt correctly to different screen sizes, with no misaligned buttons, clipping, or overlapping text.

- **Smooth Data Flow and Micro-Interactions:**
  - Ensure dynamic components (e.g., filters, quick menus) update data in real time and offer **responsive feedback** (hover states, click animations, transitions).
  - Check that user preferences (e.g., filters, accessibility modes) persist throughout navigation and function seamlessly.

---

### **4. Accessibility Compliance & Feature Testing**

- **Screen Reader & ARIA Testing:**
  - Verify ARIA roles and labels are implemented on every interactive element. Use screen reader tools to confirm compatibility with assistive technologies.
  - Ensure `alt` texts and descriptions for icons and buttons are clear yet hidden (avoiding visual clutter).

- **Additional Accessibility Modes:**
  - Ensure toggles like **high contrast mode, colorblind-friendly palettes, and text-to-speech** work consistently.
  - Create a settings sub-page for customizing advanced accessibility features:
    - Colorblind-friendly modes (Protanopia, Deuteranopia, Tritanopia).
    - Speech speed and voice options for text-to-speech.
    - Customizable cursors for enhanced visibility.

- **Keyboard Navigation:**  
  - Confirm the app is fully keyboard-navigable, with logical tab sequences and focus highlights.

---

### **5. Error Identification and Missing Features**

- **Technical Audit:**
  - Identify broken links, buttons, or missing pages (e.g., 404 errors, incomplete links).
  - Check JavaScript console for any errors or network failures during runtime.

- **Dynamic Content and Sorting Tools:**
  - Ensure that all sorting tools, filters, modals, and quick views are correctly implemented and accessible.
  - Test pagination and sorting functions for large datasets, ensuring smooth scrolling/loading.

---

### **6. Future-Proofing & Improvements**

- **Scalability and Sidebar Enhancements:**
  - Prepare the sidebar for **future modular expansion** by implementing:
    - A shortcut to the **Mood Capsules Dashboard** (icon + label).
    - Drag-and-drop modular widgets for customizable actions and quick access.
  - Maintain consistency in collapsible menu behavior and sidebar aesthetic.

- **User Experience Upgrades:**
  - Recommend UI refinements like adding subtle micro-interactions (tooltips, animations) where needed.
  - Intelligently link buttons with contextual actions based on expected user workflows, ensuring a streamlined experience.

---

### **7. Documentation, Reporting & Iterative Testing**

- **Error Documentation:**
  - Create a detailed report listing all issues found during the audit (broken links, inconsistencies, missing features).
  - Categorize findings by priority: critical, major, minor.

- **Evidence and Recommendations:**
  - Attach code snippets for identified issues and screenshots/videos showcasing UI/UX errors or broken flows.
  - Provide actionable recommendations for improvement, prioritizing usability and accessibility.

- **Iterative Feedback Plan:**
  - Propose a schedule for periodic iterative testing to incorporate fixes and enhancements based on user feedback.
